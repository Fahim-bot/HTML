What is Food Wastage?

Food wastage refers to the food that is discarded or left uneaten at various stages of the food supply chain. This includes food wasted during production, processing, distribution, and consumption. Reducing food waste is crucial for a sustainable future.

Statistics and Facts

  • Approximately 1.3 billion tons of food is wasted globally each year.
  • In the United States alone, about 30-40% of the food supply is wasted.
  • Reducing food waste can help combat climate change by reducing greenhouse gas emissions from landfills.

Impact of Food Wastage

Food wastage has significant environmental, economic, and social impacts. Environmentally, it contributes to the overuse of water and land resources and increases methane emissions from landfills. Economically, it results in the loss of valuable resources and money. Socially, it highlights the disparity between food surplus and hunger.

How to Reduce Food Waste

  1. Plan your meals and make a shopping list to avoid buying excess food.
  2. Store food properly to extend its shelf life.
  3. Use leftovers creatively to reduce waste.
  4. Donate surplus food to local food banks or shelters.
  5. Compost food scraps to reduce landfill waste and create nutrient-rich soil for gardening.
